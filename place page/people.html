PEOPLE

A diverse community, a wealthy city, and a cultured history.

Paris is a densely packed, diverse city whose population boomed from 1850, reaching a peak of 2.9 million, before steadily declining after 1931 as families moved to nearby towns around the capital instead. Most of its population are originally from outside the city, with a large portion of immigrants, mostly those from countries affiliated with the European Union.

Parisians have garnered the stereotype of being snobbish, stuck-up, or downright rude, but testimony from some people online attribute it to a clash of culture and social expectations, as well as the general busyness of being a metropolitan city.

One cultural divide is the decorum of language, and how many tourists fail to adopt the appropriate respect and apologia when addressing, or interacting around, strangers. Another is the expectations of service from waiters. France in general has a very formal culture, whereas many tourists, who mostly come from western countries, are much more casual.
