PARIS

Sitting against the bank of the river Seine is la Ville Lumière, the City of Light.

The 'City of Light' nickname is one Paris earned during the Enlightenment for both for its early adoption of extensive streetlights, harbouring stars in every lamppost to guide you as you walk through the cobbled streets, and for the leading role it provided in the arts and sciences that has imbued every aspect of the city with distinct cultural achievements to experience.

To this day, Paris upholds its reputation as a beacon that attracts a diverse array of people from all across the world to dream of one day visiting it at the centre of the Île-de-France
region. From museums, architecture, culture, gastronomy, fashion, and more - there seems to be an endless variety that's sure to lure in the dreamers and exceed expectations.
