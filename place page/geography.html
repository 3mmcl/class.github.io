VILLE

❝ Fluctuat nec mergitur ❞
She is tossed by the waves, but does not sink.

The city is surprisingly small for its dense population, only extending as much as 6 miles (10km) from the square in front of the Notre-Dame Cathedral with rivers cutting through the spaces. Their banks, as well as the city streets and stone walls, are lush with trees, ivy, and other greenery to give the city a garden-esc feel that settles it nicely in the vast forests that surround it.

Mostly beech and oak, the hills are populated with forests and forest parks which are dubbed 'the lungs of Paris', aiding in purifying the air of the very industrialised region and giving the city a refreshing feeling of life.

Due to its relatively compact size, it's possible to get around Paris by walking, but if your feet grow weary or the weather is unpleasant, the Metro is a reliable and quick way to get where you need, or simply want, to be.
