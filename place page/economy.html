EMPLOI

Paris is a major commerce centre filled with opportunity for both national and international businesses.

Traditionally, its industries focused mainly on luxury goods, but through the construction of canals and railways, it expanded to heavier industries. The Seine remains both a tourist attraction and prominent barge traffic to move downstream to ports such as Rouen and Le Havre.

Because most major French banks and other financial institutes are primarily located in Paris, finance has also become an important part of Paris' economy alongside other services such as conventions and shopping centres. The modern department store was first invented by the French and many are scattered all throughout the busy city streets and more suburban areas alike.

These factors have led the population of France, Paris in particular, to have occupations in business, service, and an above-average percent in administration and research.
